Timo Freiesleben


I really enjoy teaching! It's the best way to lose the narrow and singular mindset that you sometimes have in research. Teaching is about putting research topics into context, giving content an intuitive structure, and discussing ideas with students. In recent years I have taught several courses, both for philosophy and statistics students:

  • What to learn from the impossible? Formal and philosophical topics of impossibility theorems., summer term 24/25, philosophy, cognitive science, and computer science department, syllabus.
  • Maths primer for mathematical philsophy students, winter term 24/25 and winter term 25/26, philosophy department, script.
  • Explainable Artificial Intelligence, winter term 21/22, philosophy and statistics department, syllabus.
  • Causality and Machine Learning, summer term 21, statistics department, syllabus.
  • Philosophy of Artificial Intelligence, winter term 20/21, philosophy department, syllabus.
  • Ethics of Artificial Intelligence, summer term 20, statistics department, syllabus.

I also helped design the lecture 'Interpretable Machine Learning' at the LMU statistics department. Previously, I have also assisted in various different courses:

  • Models and Simulations, summer term 20, philosophy department, syllabus.
  • Central Topics in Philosophy of Science (CTPS), philosophy department, syllabus.
  • Linear Algebra I, II and Analysis I, II, 2016-2019, mathematics department.

In the courses I have taught, I have created some additional materials, such as a small introductory Youtube course on Netlogo , a formal script. for the CTPS course, and a simple example for students who want to get started with Latex.
